# 🛡️ 'Stay Safe Online' Checklist: College Edition (India 2026)
*A Digital Ambassador’s guide to protecting your data and finances.*

### 🔐 Account & Privacy Essentials
* [ ] **Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Use app-based authenticators (like Google or Microsoft Authenticator) for your Gmail, Instagram, and University portals. Avoid relying solely on SMS OTPs.
* [ ] **The 3-Month App Purge:** Delete unused apps and review permissions for those you keep. A "Flashlight" or "Calculator" app has no legitimate reason to access your contacts or location.
* [ ] **Public Wi-Fi Protocol:** Never access banking apps or enter passwords on college canteen or cafe Wi-Fi. If you must use public networks, always use a trusted **VPN** to encrypt your traffic.
* [ ] **Mask Your Aadhaar:** When providing ID for hostel or SIM registration, use a **'Masked Aadhaar'** (downloaded from UIDAI) which hides the first 8 digits of your number.

### 💸 Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory)
* [ ] **The "PIN is for Giving" Rule:** Always remember—**you do not need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money.** If someone sends you a QR code to "claim a prize" or "receive a refund" that asks for your PIN, it is a scam.
* [ ] **Verify the "Recipient Bank Name":** Before hitting 'Pay' on any UPI app, check the **official bank name** displayed by the NPCI's AI-alert system. If the name doesn't match the person/store you are paying, cancel immediately.
* [ ] **Set Transaction Limits:** Log into your banking or UPI app and set a "Daily UPI Transaction Limit" (e.g., ₹5,000). This ensures that even if your phone is compromised, your entire account balance isn't at risk.

### 📱 Social & Communication Safety
* [ ] **Pause the "Urgency":** Scammers often use "Deepfake" voice notes or urgent messages from "Friends in Trouble." Always call the person on a known number to verify before sending money or private info.
* [ ] **Report, Don't Engage:** If you encounter a "Burn Page" or cyberbullying, do not comment. Take a screenshot for evidence and report the profile immediately using the platform's tools.

## 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India
If you have been scammed or harassed, speed is critical—especially for financial recovery (the "Golden Hour").

1.  **Financial Fraud:** Call the National Helpline at **1930** immediately to freeze the transaction.
2.  **All Cybercrimes:** Lodge a formal complaint at the **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal**: [cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in).
3.  **Local Help:** Visit your nearest **Cyber Cell** or Police Station with screenshots and transaction IDs (UTR numbers).

> **Ambassador Note:** Reporting is not a cause for shame; it is a brave step that protects the entire community.
