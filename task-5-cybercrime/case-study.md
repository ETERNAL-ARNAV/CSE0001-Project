# Case Study: The "Anonymous Confessions" Burn Page
**Digital Ambassador Awareness Resource**

### 1. The Crime: Cyber-Harassment & Outing
This scenario involves **Cyberbullying**, specifically through **Harassment** (repeatedly sending offensive messages) and **Outing** (sharing secrets or embarrassing information online without consent).

### 2. Step-by-Step Escalation
* **The Setup:** An anonymous "Confessions" or "Tea" account is created on a platform like Instagram or TikTok, often using a school’s name to gain followers.
* **The Bait:** The admin posts an anonymous link (e.g., NGL or Tellonym), inviting peers to submit "rumors" or "secrets" about classmates.
* **The Attack:** The admin selects a target and posts a screenshot of a malicious, often fabricated, submission.
* **The Dogpile:** Peers engage by liking, sharing, or adding hateful comments, creating a viral cycle of public humiliation.

### 3. Typical Targets
Targets are often students who are **socially prominent**, **perceived as different** (due to identity, orientation, or disability), or those involved in a minor interpersonal conflict that the bully "amplifies" for views.

### 4. The Consequences
* **Victim:** Severe psychological distress, social isolation, and "school refusal" (chronic absenteeism).
* **Perpetrator:** School suspension, loss of athletic eligibility, and potentially **criminal charges** for harassment or hate speech.
* **Community:** A toxic culture of fear where students feel unsafe sharing any personal information online.

> **Ambassador Tip:** Reporting a post is a silent, powerful way to stop the "Dogpile."
